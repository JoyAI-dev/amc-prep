## Official MAA Scope Definition

> "The AMC 8 is a 25-question, 40-minute competition designed for students in grade 8 and below. The competition focuses on **middle school mathematics topics**, including counting and probability, estimation, proportional reasoning, elementary geometry (including the Pythagorean Theorem), spatial visualization, and interpreting graphs and tables. Some of the later questions may touch on beginning algebra topics, such as linear or quadratic functions and coordinate geometry."
>
> — MAA official website (maa.org/student-programs/amc/)

**Format:** 25 multiple-choice questions (5 options each), 40 minutes
**Scoring:** 25 points total; +1 correct, 0 incorrect/blank (no penalty)
**Eligibility:** Grade 8 or below, ≤14.5 years old on day of competition
**Schedule:** Annually in January over the course of a week

## Topic Categories

### 1. Geometry
**Official scope:** Elementary geometry including the Pythagorean Theorem, spatial visualization

**Sub-topics:**
- Area and perimeter (triangles, rectangles, circles, composite shapes)
- Pythagorean Theorem and its applications
- Similarity and congruence of figures
- Transformations (reflections, rotations, translations)
- Angle relationships (vertical angles, supplementary, complementary, parallel lines)
- Coordinate geometry (basic: plotting, distance, midpoint)
- 3D geometry: surface area and volume of basic solids (cubes, rectangular prisms, cylinders)
- Spatial visualization (unfolding 3D shapes, cross-sections)
- Circles: area, circumference, arc length, central angles

**Typical question count:** 7–10 of 25 (largest category, ~36% in 2025)

---

### 2. Number Theory
**Official scope:** Implicit (middle school math); explicitly included in AMC 10 as "introductory number theory"

**Sub-topics:**
- Divisibility rules (2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10)
- Factors and multiples; GCD and LCM
- Prime numbers and prime factorization
- Remainders and modular arithmetic
- Properties of even/odd numbers
- Digit problems (sum of digits, place value manipulation)
- Perfect squares and perfect cubes
- Number sequences (arithmetic, geometric, pattern recognition)

**Typical question count:** 4–6 of 25 (~20% in 2025)

---

### 3. Algebra / Word Problems
**Official scope:** Proportional reasoning, estimation; later problems may touch on linear or quadratic functions

**Sub-topics:**
- Setting up and solving linear equations (one or two variables)
- Ratio and proportion
- Percentages (increase, decrease, of a number, reverse)
- Rate problems: distance = rate × time (D=RT)
- Work rate problems
- Mixture problems
- Unit conversion and dimensional analysis
- Multi-step word problem translation (real-world contexts)
- Basic quadratic reasoning (when appearing in later questions)

**Typical question count:** 5–7 of 25 (~24% in 2025)

---

### 4. Counting & Combinatorics
**Official scope:** Counting and probability

**Sub-topics:**
- Fundamental counting principle
- Permutations (ordered arrangements)
- Combinations (unordered selections)
- Casework counting
- Complementary counting
- Counting paths on grids
- Inclusion-exclusion (basic)
- Symmetry arguments in counting
- Binomial coefficients (Pascal's triangle applications)

**Typical question count:** 2–4 of 25 (~8% in 2025)

---

### 5. Probability & Statistics
**Official scope:** Counting and probability; interpreting graphs and tables

**Sub-topics:**
- Basic probability (single and compound events)
- Complementary probability
- Expected value (basic)
- Mean, median, mode, range
- Weighted averages
- Reading and interpreting bar graphs, pie charts, line graphs, tables
- Venn diagrams and set-based probability

**Typical question count:** 3–4 of 25 (~12% in 2025)

## Difficulty Progression Within an Exam

| Questions | Difficulty | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Q1–Q10 | Easy | Direct application of single concepts |
| Q11–Q20 | Medium | Multi-step, some concept blending |
| Q21–Q25 | Hard | Multi-concept, non-obvious approaches required |

The hardest questions (Q21–25) typically require:
- Connecting two or more topic areas
- Non-obvious setup or model (draw a diagram, set up cleverly)
- Insight-based shortcuts (symmetry, invariants, casework reduction)

## Topics Explicitly Excluded from AMC 8

- Trigonometry (sin, cos, tan)
- Advanced algebra (systems with 3+ variables, complex inequalities)
- Calculus
- Logarithms
- Sequences beyond arithmetic/geometric (e.g., recurrences)
- Advanced number theory (e.g., Fermat's Little Theorem, CRT)

*These appear in AMC 10/12 and above.*
